# 1. Purpose

This document defines how council departments collaborate when managing public infrastructure.

Although every Asset belongs to a single owning department, operational work may involve multiple departments throughout its lifecycle.

InfraTrack supports this collaboration while preserving clear operational accountability and complete historical traceability.

---

# 2. Business Reality

Australian Local Governments are organised into specialised departments responsible for different operational domains.

Typical departments include:

* Parks;
* Roads;
* Facilities;
* Waste;
* Fleet;
* Building Maintenance.

In practice, operational work rarely remains confined to a single department.

A department may own an Asset while inspections, maintenance activities or specialist work are performed by another department or by external contractors.

Successful asset management therefore depends on effective collaboration between operational teams.

---

# 3. InfraTrack Decision

InfraTrack distinguishes between ownership, operational responsibility and task assignment.

These concepts are intentionally independent.

Owning a public asset does not imply performing every operational activity related to that asset.

Likewise, performing operational work does not transfer ownership of the Asset.

This separation reflects the operational reality of Australian councils while preserving accountability.

---

# 4. Ownership and Operational Responsibility

## Asset Ownership

Every Asset belongs to exactly one owning department.

The owning department remains accountable for the Asset throughout its operational lifecycle.

Ownership does not change simply because another department performs operational work.

---

## Operational Responsibility

Operational activities may be performed by authorised personnel from another department.

Examples include:

* inspections;
* maintenance activities;
* specialist assessments;
* emergency interventions.

The department performing the work becomes operationally responsible for that specific activity only.

---

## Task Assignment

Operational work is always assigned to individual users rather than departments.

Users perform the work assigned to them regardless of the owning department of the Asset.

Task assignment determines who performs the work.

It does not modify ownership or departmental accountability.

---

# 5. Cross-Department Collaboration

Managers may access operational information across departments in order to support council-wide coordination.

Operational decisions affecting another department should only be made when responsibility has been explicitly delegated or assigned according to council policy.

Cross-department collaboration should improve operational continuity without creating uncertainty regarding accountability.

---

# 6. Delegated Authority

Operational responsibility may be temporarily delegated when required.

Examples include:

* manager absence;
* emergency situations;
* shared operational responsibilities;
* cross-department operational support.

Delegation grants temporary authority to perform operational actions.

It does not transfer permanent ownership of the Asset.

The delegated authority should remain traceable within the operational history.

---

                Asset

                  │
        (if delegated)

                  │
                  ▼

This example illustrates that ownership, operational responsibility and task assignment remain distinct throughout the operational lifecycle.

---

# 8. Guiding Principles

## Every Asset has one owning department.

Ownership establishes long-term accountability.

---

## Operational work may cross departmental boundaries.

Departments collaborate whenever operational requirements demand it.

---

## Task assignment determines operational access.

Employees perform work assigned to them regardless of departmental ownership.

---

## Delegation determines cross-department authority.

Managers may act outside their department only when responsibility has been explicitly delegated or assigned.

---

## Collaboration must remain traceable.

InfraTrack records both the owning department and the department or user responsible for each operational activity.

---

# 9. Relationship with Asset History

Every operational event records who performed the work and under which organisational responsibility it occurred.

Asset History therefore preserves:

* the owning department;
* the department responsible for each operational activity;
* the assigned user;
* delegated authority, when applicable.

This historical information supports transparency, accountability and future operational reviews.

---

# Summary

## Business Reality

Council departments collaborate continuously to inspect, maintain and manage public infrastructure.

Operational work frequently crosses departmental boundaries while ownership remains unchanged.

---

## InfraTrack Decision

InfraTrack separates Asset ownership, operational responsibility, task assignment and delegated authority.

This approach reflects real-world council operations while preserving accountability, collaboration and complete operational traceability.
