# 1. Purpose

This document defines how notifications support operational communication within InfraTrack.

Notifications exist to inform users that a business event requires their attention.

They improve operational awareness but do not replace business processes.

Notifications are communication mechanisms, not business records.

---

# 2. Business Reality

Australian Local Governments coordinate operational activities across multiple teams, departments and external contractors.

Operational work often requires timely communication to ensure that inspections, maintenance activities and reviews are performed efficiently.

Typical situations requiring notification include:

* assignment of operational work;
* completion of maintenance activities;
* requests for administrative review;
* urgent operational events;
* synchronisation failures for field staff.

Without timely communication, operational delays may occur even when business processes have been correctly recorded.

---

# 3. InfraTrack Decision

InfraTrack treats notifications as communication generated by business events.

Notifications never initiate business processes.

Instead, they inform users that a business event has already occurred and may require action.

The business event remains the source of truth.

Notifications exist solely to improve operational coordination.

---

# 4. Notification Events

Notifications may be generated by significant operational events.

Typical events include:

* Inspection Assigned;
* Work Order Assigned;
* Work Order Updated;
* Contractor Assignment;
* Maintenance Completed;
* Completion Review Required;
* Urgent Operational Request;
* Synchronisation Failure.

Additional notification events may be introduced as the platform evolves.

---

# 5. Notification Recipients

Notifications are delivered to the users or business roles responsible for responding to the associated business event.

Typical recipients include:

* Field Employees;
* Managers;
* Operational Coordinators;
* Contractors;
* Administrators (system-related notifications only).

The recipient depends on the nature of the business event rather than the notification itself.

---

# 6. Notification Channels

InfraTrack may deliver notifications through different communication channels.

Version 1 supports:

* Dashboard Notifications;
* Email Notifications;
* Mobile Push Notifications.

The communication channel should be appropriate for the urgency and context of the operational event.

Future delivery channels may be introduced without changing the underlying business model.

---

# 7. Notification Lifecycle

Notifications follow a simple communication lifecycle.

```text

Business Event

 │
 ▼

Notification Created

 │
 ▼

Delivered

 │
 ▼

Viewed

 │
 ▼

Dismissed

```

Dismissing a notification does not affect the underlying business event.

Operational work remains unchanged regardless of the notification status.

---

# 8. Notification Rules

## Business events generate notifications.

Notifications always originate from an existing business event.

They never exist independently.

---

## Notifications do not replace operational work.

Receiving or dismissing a notification does not create, modify or complete any business activity.

---

## Notifications reference business events.

Every notification should reference the operational event that generated it.

Users must always be able to navigate from a notification to its associated business record.

---

## Notifications may be delivered through multiple channels.

The same notification may be presented through several communication channels when appropriate.

This does not create multiple business events.

---

## Notification delivery is not guaranteed.

Temporary delivery failures should not affect the underlying business process.

Operational records remain authoritative regardless of notification status.

---

# 10. Relationship with Asset History

Notifications are not part of an Asset's operational history.

The business event that generated the notification is preserved within the Asset History.

Notifications are temporary communication records intended to support operational awareness.

Historical traceability is provided by the underlying business events rather than the notifications themselves.

---

# Summary

## Business Reality

Operational teams require timely communication to coordinate inspections, maintenance activities and reviews.

Notifications improve operational awareness but do not represent operational work.

---

## InfraTrack Decision

InfraTrack models notifications as communication generated by business events.

Business events remain the authoritative operational record, while notifications provide temporary awareness and coordination for the appropriate users.
